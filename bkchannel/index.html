An Ion Channel Story

BK Channels

BK channels, short for "big potassium" channels, are specialized proteins that control the flow of potassium ions across cell membranes (ion channels). These channels are like tiny gatekeepers: when conditions are right, the channel opens, allowing potassium ions to flow out of the cell, thus regulating ion movement, which is essential for processes like muscle contraction, nerve signaling, and blood pressure regulation.

POPC Lipids

POPC, or 1-palmitoyl-2-oleoyl-sn-glycero-3-phosphocholine, lipids are a type of molecule that plays a critical role in biology. These lipids are a major component of cell membranes, forming flexible yet sturdy barriers that protect and support cellular function.
