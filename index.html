Hey there, wanderer of the internet! I'm Lucia and I'm currently navigating the labyrinth of academia as a postdoc in the Lab of Professor Vincenzo Carnevale at the Institute for Computational Molecular Science (ICMS) at Temple University. My days are a delightful mishmash of decoding the secret lives of ion channels, throwing compounds and proteins into the mix just to see what happens. Well, actually there is a goal behind and it's trying to figure out how those thing work mechanically, and once you know it, you rule it.

AND there's more! Ever heard of DNA origami? No, it's not a quirky craft project. I'm knee-deep in the design and study of metamaterials that can self-assemble, and yes, it involves DNA. It's like nature's LEGO, only way cooler.

And because multitasking is my middle name (not really), I also supervise drug design projects. Because, you know, why stop at unraveling the mysteries of molecular structures when you can also cook up the next big thing in medicine?

For those who like their facts straight up, Here's my resume. It's the CliffsNotes version of my rollercoaster journey through the scientific wonderland.

So, welcome to my little corner of the web. If you're into ion channels, metamaterials, and the occasional drug design escapade, you're in the right place. Everything's a bit wacky and wonderful. Stick around, and let's embark on this scientific adventure together!
